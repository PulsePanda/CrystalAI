Hi Michael,

Thanks for reaching out. The internet light being off usually means the board lost its network connection — likely a loose ethernet cable or the board needs a proper hard reboot (the remote doesn't always fully reset the network).

A few things to try before we get someone out there:

1. Check the ethernet cable at the back of the board and the wall jack — give both ends a firm push to reseat them. A partially pulled cable is the most common culprit.

2. Try a full hard power cycle: use the physical power button on the board itself (not the remote), hold it until the board shuts off completely, wait about 30 seconds, then power it back on. The remote doesn't always reset the network stack the same way.

3. If the board connects via Wi-Fi instead of ethernet, go to Settings > Network and see if it's showing as connected with an IP address.

If none of those work, let me know and I'll get someone to Room 214 to take a look — I want to make sure you're back up and running before the end of the day.
