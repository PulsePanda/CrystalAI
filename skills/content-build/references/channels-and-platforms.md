## Platform-Specific Writing Rules

### Twitter
- 280 character limit per tweet
- Thread format for longer ideas: first tweet is the hook, subsequent tweets expand
- Hashtags: 1-3 specific ones (#SchoolIT, #EdTech, #ERate, #K12IT, #MSP, #ChartSchools)
- No links in tweet body if possible — link in reply
- Punchy, direct, hook-driven. Every tweet should stand alone as value.

### LinkedIn
- ~1300 character practical max (gets cut off after ~1500)
- Hook in the FIRST LINE — must earn the "...see more" click
- Specificity over inspiration — concrete stories, real numbers, genuine takes
- Have a take — a position worth defending
- No external links in post body — LinkedIn suppresses them. "Link in comments."
- 3-5 specific hashtags (#SchoolIT over #Technology, #ERate over #Funding)
- One idea per paragraph. Max 2-3 lines per paragraph. White space is engagement.
- Break at tension points to force "see more"
- CTA that invites a reply: "What would you add?" beats "Like if you agree"

### Facebook
- No strict length limit but keep it readable
- Slightly more casual, community-oriented tone
- Questions and calls for engagement work well
- Links OK in body (Facebook doesn't suppress them like LinkedIn)
- Umbrella page only — no personal Facebook posting

## Voice Framing Guide

### Umbrella Voice
- Third person / company "we"
- Capability-focused: "How we help schools..."
- Professional but warm, not corporate
- Positions Umbrella as the expert partner
- Case studies framed as client wins

### Austin Voice
- First person "I"
- Experience-focused: "What I've learned..."
- Opinionated, story-driven, honest about mistakes
- Positions Austin as the individual expert
- War stories, lessons learned, genuine takes
- Building-in-public content (AI, automation, Claude Code) lives here exclusively
