## GIS — thorough policy explanation to teacher

**Context:** Explaining Grammarly policy to teacher concerned about student use during tests. Shows: addresses each point, states real options, admits complexity, separates opinion from policy.

> Hi Kat,
>
> I was just writing up a response to our previous email but I'll send it here instead.
>
> Grammarly is enabled for the students citing accessibility - specifically it's been requested multiple times by various staff including the SpEd team - specifically for its assistance in proofreading an assignment for submission. For reference, other schools use Grammarly too for this purpose.
>
> The issue isn't that it's difficult to remove a chrome extension, the issue is that it's a tool the school is/has been using. You're correct in that students aren't allowed to add or modify software to their chromebooks, but that's not the same as installing an explicitly allowed extension to their device.
>
> I think the big issue here is because Grammarly is an installed application, GoGuardian can't block its use when in lockdown mode. But, GoGuardian does see that it's being used, and reports on it.
>
> The way I see it there are two solutions - either we remove Grammarly from all student devices and tell those that want it that it's not allowed anymore - which I can totally do and field the complaints for. Or, we make it clear to the students that the use of Grammarly on this or other assignments is not allowed and that when GoGuardian shows they used it, they'll receive a worse grade or an F or something.
